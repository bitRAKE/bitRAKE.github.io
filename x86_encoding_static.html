Legacy Pfx · 0–4 bytes
Up to 4 prefixes in any order: • LOCK (F0) • REP/REPNE (F2/F3) • Segment override (26 2E 36 3E 64 65) • Operand-size (66) • Address-size (67)
REX · 40h–4Fh
REX.WRXB (one byte): • W – 64-bit operand size • R – extends ModRM.reg to 4 bits • X – extends SIB.index • B – extends ModRM.r/m or opcode-embedded reg
Opcode · 1 byte

ModRM · 1 byte

SIB · 1 byte

Field Notes
Legacy Pfx · 0–4 bytes
Includes address-size override (67h) and operand-size override (66h). These are still legal with legacy encodings and can change decoding rules.
REX · 40h–4Fh
REX.WRXB (one byte). Traps here: B extends base (ModRM.r/m or SIB.base), X extends SIB.index.
Opcode · 1 byte
Example opcodes here are MOV (8Bh/89h). Structure is the point — not the mnemonic.
ModRM · 1 byte
mod(2) · reg(3) · r/m(3) Key traps: • mod≠11 & r/m=100b → SIB is present • mod=00 & r/m=101b → disp32 (RIP-relative in 64-bit mode) • base=RBP/R13 cannot use mod=00 without a disp
SIB · 1 byte
scale(2) · index(3) · base(3) Key traps: • base=100b selects RSP/R12 (and often forces SIB) • index=100b means “no index” (not RSP) • base=101b with mod=00 implies disp32 (no base)
Disp · 0/1/2/4B
Many addressing traps resolve by emitting disp8=00 (a real byte) to avoid the mod=00 special cases.

Not used in these examples.

Field Notes
C5h · VEX 2B marker
VEX 2-byte prefix marker C5h. All legacy prefixes and REX are FORBIDDEN. The payload encodes what REX/mandatory-prefix would have been. In 32-bit mode 0xC5 can also start LDS; VEX2 is recognized when the following byte matches the VEX2 payload pattern (bits[7:6]=11).
R̄ · v̄v̄v̄v̄ · L · pp · 1 payload byte
One payload byte, 8 bits: • [7] R̄ inverted REX.R • [6:3] v̄v̄v̄v̄ inverted vvvv — 2nd source register • [2] L 0=128b XMM, 1=256b YMM • [1:0] pp implied mandatory prefix: 00=none 01=66h 10=F3h 11=F2h Map 1 only. No REX.W / X / B → use 3-byte for those.
Opcode · map 1
Opcode from map 1 (equivalent to 0F xx). Destination in ModRM.reg, extended by R̄.

Field Notes
62h · EVEX marker
EVEX marker 62h. In 64-bit mode BOUND is removed, freeing 62h. REX and VEX strictly forbidden. Total instruction length ≤ 15 bytes.
P0 · P1 · P2 · 3 payload bytes
P0: R̄ X̄ B̄ R̄' · 00 · mm • R̄X̄B̄ – inverted REX.RXB • R̄' – extends reg to 5 bits (32 regs) • mm: 01=map1, 02=map2, 03=map3, 05=MAP5, 06=MAP6 P1: W · v̄v̄v̄v̄ · 1 · pp • W – operand size / element type • vvvv – inverted NDS register • pp – implied mandatory prefix P2: z · L' · L · b · V̄' · aaa • z – 0=merge masking, 1=zero masking • L'L – 00=XMM, 01=YMM, 10=ZMM • b – memory: broadcast; register: embedded rounding/SAE • V̄' – extends vvvv to 5 bits (32 NDS regs) • aaa – opmask: k0=no mask, k1–k7=apply mask
Opcode · maps 1–6
Opcode from selected map. MAP5/MAP6: AVX-512 FP16 (AVX10): VMOVSH, VADDPH, VCVTPH2PS…
ModRM · 1 byte
Always present. reg extended to 5 bits (R + R') → 32 ZMM registers.
SIB/VSIB · 1 byte
SIB: standard base-index-scale. VSIB: index is XMM/YMM/ZMM — used by gather/scatter: VGATHERDPS, VPGATHERDD, VSCATTERQPD…
Disp*N · 0/1*/4B
Compressed disp8*N: 1-byte disp8 scaled by tuple size N. Range: [−128N, 127N] — compact for common strides. 4-byte full displacement also available.
Imm8 · 0/1B
Optional imm8 for rounding, permute control, shift count, comparison predicate.

8Fh · XOP marker
XOP marker 8Fh. AMD-specific; no Intel support. Conflicts with POP in 32-bit mode — disambiguated by ModRM.reg ≠ 0. Deprecated: removed in AMD Zen 4 (2022).
